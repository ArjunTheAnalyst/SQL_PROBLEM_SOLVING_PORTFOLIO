# 🧮 Ratio and Proportion: Age Relationship Analysis

## Problem Statement

The ratio of the ages of A and B is 4:5, and the sum of their ages is 90 years.

What will be the ratio of their ages after 10 years?

### Options

- A) 3:4
- B) 5:6
- C) 4:5
- D) 6:7

---

## Solution Approach

### Step 1: Represent Ages Using Variables

Given:

Age(A) : Age(B) = 4 : 5

Assume:

- Age(A) = 4x
- Age(B) = 5x

Since their combined age is 90:

4x + 5x = 90

9x = 90

x = 10

---

### Step 2: Calculate Current Ages

- Age(A) = 4 × 10 = 40 years
- Age(B) = 5 × 10 = 50 years

---

### Step 3: Calculate Ages After 10 Years

- Age(A) = 40 + 10 = 50 years
- Age(B) = 50 + 10 = 60 years

---

### Step 4: Determine the New Ratio

50 : 60

Divide both values by 10:

5 : 6

---

## ✅ Final Answer

**Option B) 5 : 6**

## Key Learning

When a fixed value is added to both terms of a ratio, the ratio generally changes. Converting the ratio into actual values before applying the adjustment makes the problem straightforward to solve.
