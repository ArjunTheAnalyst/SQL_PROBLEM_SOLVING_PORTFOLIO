# 🏃 Average Speed Journey Analysis

## 📌 Overview

This exercise demonstrates how to calculate the average speed of a journey involving multiple travel segments at different speeds.

It highlights an important quantitative aptitude concept: **average speed is not simply the average of individual speeds**.

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement

Sarah decides to visit her friend who lives **120 km away**.

Her journey consists of:

- First **60 km** at **30 km/h**
- Next **60 km** at **60 km/h**

Determine her average speed for the entire journey.

### Options

- A) 42 km/h
- B) 40 km/h
- C) 43 km/h
- D) 44 km/h

---

## 📝 Solution

### Step 1: Calculate Time for Each Segment

Formula:

\[
\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}}
\]

#### First 60 km

\[
\frac{60}{30} = 2 \text{ hours}
\]

#### Next 60 km

\[
\frac{60}{60} = 1 \text{ hour}
\]

---

### Step 2: Calculate Total Time

\[
2 + 1 = 3 \text{ hours}
\]

---

### Step 3: Calculate Total Distance

\[
60 + 60 = 120 \text{ km}
\]

---

### Step 4: Calculate Average Speed

Formula:

\[
\text{Average Speed} =
\frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}}
\]

\[
=
\frac{120}{3}
\]

\[
= 40 \text{ km/h}
\]

---

## ✅ Final Answer

**40 km/h (Option B)**

---

## 🔑 Key Takeaway

For journeys involving multiple speeds:

> **Average Speed = Total Distance ÷ Total Time**

Always calculate the total travel time before determining the average speed.
