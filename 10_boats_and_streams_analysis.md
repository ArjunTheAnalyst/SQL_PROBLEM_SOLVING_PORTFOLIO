# 🚤 Boats and Streams Analysis

## 📌 Overview

This exercise demonstrates how to solve a classic Boats and Streams problem using algebra and speed-distance-time relationships.

The objective is to determine the speed of a boat in still water by using its downstream and upstream travel speeds.

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement

A boat takes:

- 4 hours to travel 40 km downstream
- 5 hours to travel 20 km upstream

Determine the speed of the boat in still water.

### Options

- A) 6 km/h
- B) 7 km/h
- C) 8 km/h
- D) 9 km/h

---

## 📝 Solution

### Step 1: Define Variables

Let:

- **B** = Speed of the boat in still water
- **C** = Speed of the current

---

### Step 2: Calculate Downstream Speed

Formula:

\[
\text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}}
\]

Downstream:

\[
\frac{40}{4} = 10 \text{ km/h}
\]

Since downstream speed equals boat speed plus current speed:

\[
B + C = 10
\]

---

### Step 3: Calculate Upstream Speed

Upstream:

\[
\frac{20}{5} = 4 \text{ km/h}
\]

Since upstream speed equals boat speed minus current speed:

\[
B - C = 4
\]

---

### Step 4: Solve the Equations

We now have:

\[
B + C = 10
\]

\[
B - C = 4
\]

Adding both equations:

\[
(B + C) + (B - C) = 10 + 4
\]

\[
2B = 14
\]

\[
B = \frac{14}{2}
\]

\[
B = 7
\]

---

## ✅ Final Answer

**7 km/h (Option B)**

## 📚 Key Formulas

### Downstream Speed

\[
\text{Boat Speed} + \text{Current Speed}
\]

### Upstream Speed

\[
\text{Boat Speed} - \text{Current Speed}
\]

### Speed

\[
\text{Distance} \div \text{Time}
\]

---

## 🔑 Key Takeaway

In Boats and Streams problems:

- Downstream travel benefits from the current.
- Upstream travel is slowed by the current.
- Converting the given information into equations makes the problem straightforward to solve.
